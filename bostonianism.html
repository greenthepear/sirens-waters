Bostonianism

It has become obvious to every rational person that Earth has either the ability or will to help us.

- Opening words to the "We are Earth's orphans" essay.

Bostonianism (known by many other names) is a belief that re-establishing contact with Earth is futile and andans should instead fully focus on establishing self-sustenance. More radical bostonians also believe that Earth has fully abandoned and betrayed Anda after the Awakening or directly blame the colonization for the Siren. The varying degree of resentment towards Earth throughout the movement also results in opposition of attempts to escape Anda.

In a more general sense the doctrine can also describe the opposition to the General Colonial Government.

Name

The name bostonian comes from the city New Boston where the "We are Earth's orphans" essay/manifesto by Petr Jogiches was written, where the ideology mainly spread from and where the capital of the bostonian Free Anda Republic lies. It can also refer to Earth's historical Boston Tea Party, which is analogous to the movement's history. Because of the essay's name orphanism is also used, as well as just andanism.

Older terminology is also common, such as: autarkism, seperatism, secessionism, anti-colonialism and jacobinism.

History

Petr Jogiches' manifesto was written in the second year of the Great Famine, as a direct response to the GCG's failed attempts to put new farming and communication infrastructure in orbit. These projects were seen as desperate and a waste of resources which could've been instead used to quell the famine. Jogiches believed that GCG's focus on reestablishing contact with Earth above all else was because of colonial incompetence, that GCG leadership was unable to act without Earth's orders. These beliefs reflected that of the general public, but especially in New Boston which was the most isolated city of its size from GCG's capital. These circumstances led to the city being the stronghold and birthplace of the Famished Uprising, and eventually the became the capital of the newly formed Free Anda Republic.

Opposition

Londonism (derived from the Boston Tea Party analogy), anti-bostonianism, earthism or loyalism is the belief that re-establishing communication with earth is the only chance for andan survival, often by the means of escaping from the planet.

Culpabilism

Culpabilism (from latin culpabilis) is the belief that human activity is to blame for the Awakening, which is an aspect of bostonian ideology. The term though is usually used to refer to derived extremist ideologies which differ in ideals on the supported response to this realization:

  • sacri-culpablisim: "the Siren is a holy entity that must be worshiped if we want to see her mercy", a common precept of new religious movements on Anda

  • post-culpabilism: "we deserve the suffering the Siren has caused, we must continue to live and suffer for it is justice on us"

  • culpa-extinctionism: "humanity needs to be removed from Anda", generally acted upon through terrorism and mass suicide in supporting groups
